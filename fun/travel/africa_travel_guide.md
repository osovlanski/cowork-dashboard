## Top 10 African Destinations

### 1. Morocco - Gateway Africa

#### Marrakech - Exotic City Center

**Highlights:**
- Medina (old walled city), souks (markets with spices, carpets, leather)
- Jemaa el-Fnaa Square (snake charmers, food stalls, performers)
- Koutoubia Mosque and palace gardens
- Nearby Atlas Mountains (hiking, Berber villages)
- Hammams (traditional steam baths)

**Flight from TLV:** 2.5 hours (direct flights available, close!)

**Best Season:** October-April (summers hot, 40+C)

**Visa for Israelis:** ISSUE - Israel-Morocco diplomatic tension. As of 2026, check current diplomatic status. Historically complicated; use travel agent or check Ministry of Foreign Affairs before booking.

**Estimated Daily Cost:**
- Budget: 50-70 USD (very affordable)
- Mid-range: 100-150 USD
- Luxury: 250+ USD

**Stay:** 2-3 days minimum

---

#### Chefchaouen - Blue City Magic

**Highlights:**
- Entire city painted bright blue (photographs incredibly)
- Berber culture and local life
- Mountain hiking trails
- Tiny medina, walkable
- Peaceful compared to Marrakech
- Local tajine restaurants

**Getting There from TLV:** Flight to Marrakech, then bus/share taxi 7 hours, OR fly to Fez (closer)

**Best Season:** October-April

**Estimated Daily Cost:**
- Budget: 40-60 USD (cheap)
- Mid-range: 80-120 USD
- Luxury: 180+ USD

**Stay:** 1-2 days

## Safari Guide: National Parks & What to Expect

### Big Five Breakdown
1. **Lion** - Most sightings during game drives (hunt at dusk/dawn)
2. **Elephant** - Frequently seen, intelligent, majestic
3. **Buffalo** - Large herds, aggressive when alone
4. **Leopard** - Rarest Big Five, solitary, nocturnal
5. **Rhino** - Endangered, hardest to spot

**Note:** "Big Five" hunting term (dangerous to hunt); not same as "Big Cats"

---

### Top Safari Destinations Ranked

**1. Serengeti (Tanzania)**
- Pros: Great Migration spectacle, diverse landscape, reasonably priced
- Cons: Crowded during migration, expensive flights
- Best For: Migration viewing, entire African experience

---

**2. Maasai Mara (Kenya)**
- Pros: Accessible from Nairobi, consistent wildlife, hot air balloons
- Cons: Often crowded, pricey compared to Tanzania
- Best For: First-time safari-goers

---

**3. Okavango Delta (Botswana)**
- Pros: Unique water-based safaris, luxury camps, wildlife abundance
- Cons: Very expensive, small aircraft required
- Best For: Luxury experience, unique ecosystem

---

**4. Kruger National Park (South Africa)**
- Pros: Affordable compared to Kenya/Tanzania, self-drive option, diverse
- Cons: Large park (hard to spot Big Five), less intimate
- Best For: Budget safari, self-paced

---

**5. Ngorongoro Crater (Tanzania)**
- Pros: High animal density, crater views, less crowded than Serengeti
- Cons: Day-visit only, limited lodging
- Best For: Day trip, high-concentration wildlife viewing

---

## Safety Guide by Region

### Egypt
**Safety Level:** MODERATE (tourist areas safe, avoid political areas)
- Avoid: Sinai Peninsula, large political gatherings, solo women at night
- Safe Areas: Giza, Cairo tourist zones, Luxor, Red Sea resorts
- Tips: Use registered taxis, don't discuss politics, cover shoulders/knees in conservative areas

---

### Morocco
**Safety Level:** SAFE (very tourist-friendly)
- Avoid: Walking alone at night in medinas
- Safe Areas: Marrakech, Chefchaouen, most tourist routes
- Tips: Haggle in markets (expected), women generally safe, some harassment possible

---

### Kenya
**Safety Level:** MODERATE (tourist areas safe, avoid Nairobi slums)
- Avoid: Walking alone after dark, remote areas without guides
- Safe Areas: Safari lodges, Nairobi CBD, coastal resorts
- Tips: Use reputable safari operators, stick to group activities in Nairobi

---

### Tanzania
**Safety Level:** SAFE (very welcoming, generally safe)
- Avoid: Dar es Salaam slums at night
- Safe Areas: Serengeti (lodges), Zanzibar, Kilimanjaro areas
- Tips: Use registered guides, tourist areas secure

---

### Ethiopia
**Safety Level:** MODERATE (improving, stick to tourist routes)
- Avoid: Remote areas, border regions, solo travel
- Safe Areas: Addis Ababa (tourist zones), Lalibela, Awash
- Tips: Use guides, group tours recommended, limited solo infrastructure

---

### South Africa
**Safety Level:** MODERATE (safe in tourist/business areas, dangerous in townships)
- Avoid: Walking alone at night, townships without guides, flashing valuables
- Safe Areas: Cape Town Waterfront, Kruger camps, established hotels
- Tips: Stay in groups at night, don't flash expensive items, carjacking awareness

---

### Rwanda
**Safety Level:** SAFE (cleanest/safest African destination)
- Avoid: None really (very secure)
- Safe Areas: Everywhere touristy
- Tips: One of Africa's safest countries; minimal concerns

---

### Jordan (Petra/Wadi Rum)
**Safety Level:** SAFE (extremely safe, very touristy)
- Avoid: None in tourist areas
- Safe Areas: Petra, Wadi Rum, Amman
- Tips: Safest Middle East destination for travelers

---

### Senegal
**Safety Level:** SAFE (most stable West African country)
- Avoid: Some theft in markets, avoid political areas
- Safe Areas: Dakar, Saint-Louis, tourist regions
- Tips: Generally very welcoming, minimal security concerns

---

### General African Safety Tips
1. **Use Registered Guides:** Book through established operators
2. **Stay in Groups:** Group activities safer than solo
3. **Avoid Night Walks:** Most cities unsafe after dark
4. **Keep Valuables Hidden:** Petty theft common in markets
5. **Trust Local Advice:** Ask hotel staff about areas
6. **Travel Insurance:** Highly recommended, costs 30-50 USD/week
7. **Medical:** Get travel vaccines (Yellow Fever, Typhoid, Hepatitis A)
8. **Backup Plans:** Keep copies of documents, backup copies of passport
