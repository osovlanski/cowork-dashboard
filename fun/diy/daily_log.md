## 2026-05-12 (Tuesday) — Quick Win: Desk Organizer from PVC Pipe 🗂️

Hey Itay! Tuesday after-work quick win — and this one is deeply on-brand for a software engineer: **Desk Organizer from PVC Pipe (Project #11)**. Modular, customizable, and weirdly satisfying to build.

**The vitals**
- Time: ~2 hours
- Budget: 150-300 ILS
- Difficulty: Medium (but easy medium — no studs, no walls, just pipes and connectors)
- Tools: PVC pipes (1.5–2 inch), T-connectors, elbows, saw or pipe cutter, sandpaper

**Tonight's 3-step game plan:**
1. Sketch your layout — how many compartments do you need? Pens, cables, a spot for headphones? One quick paper sketch is enough. Measure your desk width so you buy the right pipe lengths.
2. Grab PVC pipes and connectors from Ace Hardware or Obi on the way home — the 1.5-inch diameter is the sweet spot for most desk items.
3. Cut pipes to length, sand the cut edges, and dry-fit everything before committing. No glue needed if the fit is snug.

Optional bonus: spray paint the whole thing matte black or white before assembly — it looks custom, not hardware store. Two hours, under 200 ILS, zero wall damage. Perfect rental build. 🔧

## 2026-05-08 (Friday) — Quick Win: Phone/Tablet Stand from Wood 📱

TGIF, Itay! Friday quick win, and today's pick is one of those small builds that punches way above its weight: **Phone/Tablet Stand from Wood (Project #6)**. Cheap, fast, and you'll use it every single day for video calls and cooking videos.

**The vitals**
- Time: 1-2 hours
- Budget: 80-150 ILS (less if you've got wood scraps)
- Difficulty: Easy
- Tools: Wood offcuts, saw, sandpaper, drill, screws, felt pads

**Tonight's 3-step game plan**
1. Grab two wood offcuts (~15cm × 5cm each) — Ace Hardware sells small pine pieces for under 20 ILS, or check a local carpenter for free scraps.
2. Sand all edges smooth, then drill pilot holes and join the two pieces at a 45° angle with wood screws — one vertical base, one angled cradle.
3. Stick felt pads on the bottom (protects desk + adds grip), and add a thin rubber strip or carve a shallow groove on the cradle so your phone won't slide.

Optional polish: stain or oil the wood to match your desk. The whole build is under two hours and gives you a custom stand that beats the 80-shekel plastic ones at any office store. Engineer-grade output, cooking-show-grade utility. 🎬

Shabbat shalom in advance! 🕯️

## 2026-05-07 (Thursday) — Start a New Project: Window Insulation Film ☀️

Morning, Itay! Thursday = new project day, and the timing on this one is *chef's kiss*: **Window Insulation Film for Summer (Project #7)**. May is when Tel Aviv apartments start cooking — get this up now and you'll feel the difference on the very next hot afternoon. Bonus: less screen glare during work.

**The vitals**
- Time: 1-2 hours per window
- Budget: 300-500 ILS for 3-4 windows
- Difficulty: Medium
- Tools: Window film, spray bottle, squeegee, utility knife, dish soap

**First 3 steps to get going today**
1. Measure your window panes exactly — width × height for each one. Make a list so you only buy what you need (and grab a bit extra; trimming is way easier than stretching).
2. Order or pick up reflective window film — Ace Hardware, Obi, or AliExpress all carry it. Get the heat-rejecting kind, not just privacy film.
3. Cut each piece slightly larger than its pane (about 1-2cm overhang on each side). Mix water + a few drops of dish soap in a spray bottle so it's ready for installation night.

If tonight is a 20-minute night, just do steps 1 and 2 — the install itself is the satisfying part and works great as a Saturday morning task. Cooler apartment + lower AC bill = engineer math that checks out. ❄️

## 2026-05-05 (Tuesday, alt pick) — Quick Win: Kitchen Herb Garden 🌿

Morning, Itay! Already had Cable Management on the menu for today — here's an alternate quick win in case that one's done (or you just want something different): **Grow Your Own Herbs (Project #3)**. May in Tel Aviv is *the* sweet spot for this — warm enough that everything sprouts fast, not yet brutal August heat.

**The vitals**
- Time: ~1 hour setup (then 2 minutes a day to water)
- Budget: 250-400 ILS
- Difficulty: Easy
- Tools: Planters with drainage, potting soil, seeds or seedlings, watering can

**Tonight's 3-step game plan:**
1. On the way home, swing by a nursery (משתלה) or even a supermarket with a garden section — grab basil, mint, parsley, and coriander seedlings (skip seeds if you want results this week).
2. Fill 4 planters with potting soil (NOT garden soil — drainage matters), pop in the seedlings, water gently until soil is moist.
3. Park them on the sunniest spot of your balcony or kitchen window — 6+ hours of sun is the magic number.

Once they're up, pinch off any flower buds you see — that's how you keep the leaves coming all summer. Fresh basil for pasta, mint for the inevitable hot summer iced tea, coriander for shakshuka. Engineer-grade ROI per square cm of balcony. 🌱

## 2026-05-05 (Tuesday) — Quick Win: Cable Management Desk Setup 🖥️

Hey Itay! Tuesday night quick win — no trips to Ace required for this one.

**Cable Management Desk Setup (Project #2)** is exactly the kind of thing you keep meaning to do and then don't — but once it's done, you'll wonder how you ever lived with the spaghetti. Takes an hour, maybe less.

**The vitals**
- Time: 1-2 hours
- Budget: 150-300 ILS (or free if you've got velcro/cable ties lying around)
- Difficulty: Easy
- Tools: Cable ties or velcro straps, cable clips, scissors, masking tape for labels

**Tonight's 3-step game plan:**
1. Grab every cable on your desk and sort them by function — power, USB, display. Bundle each group with a velcro strap or cable tie.
2. Mount cable clips under the desk edge using adhesive (Command strips work great and are rental-safe).
3. Route each bundle through the clips, label them with a tiny piece of tape, and tuck the excess behind the desk.

If your power strip is sitting on the floor, velcro-mounting it to the underside of the desk is the single highest-ROI move. Clean floor, clean desk, cleaner mind. Take a before/after photo — you'll want it. 🔌

## 2026-05-02 (Saturday) — Full Project Day: Balcony Privacy Screen 🌿

Shabbat shalom, Itay! Saturday = full project day, and May in Tel Aviv is *perfect* timing for this one: **Balcony Privacy Screen (Project #8)**. Get this done today and you've basically unlocked a private outdoor room for the whole summer.

**The vitals**
- Time: 2-3 hours
- Budget: 400-700 ILS
- Difficulty: Medium
- Tools: 2x2cm wood, privacy slats or woven material, drill, screws, saw

**All the steps**
1. Measure your balcony dimensions — width and desired height.
2. Build a simple wooden frame from 2x2cm wood, cut to size.
3. Join corners with screws at 90° angles.
4. Check the frame is square using a diagonal measurement.
5. Attach privacy slats (horizontal boards) or woven privacy material.
6. Secure to the balcony railing with screws or brackets.
7. Add extra brackets so it won't blow away in the hamsin winds.
8. Stain or paint to taste — natural wood looks great in TLV light.

**Tip**: Obi has pre-cut 2x2cm wood that saves you the saw work. Horizontal slats = modern look; woven mesh = budget-friendly. Coffee on the balcony at sunset tonight is the reward. ☕

## 2026-04-25 (Saturday) — Full Project Day: Wall-Mounted Floating Shelves

Saturday energy! Today's pick is the most satisfying medium-difficulty project on the list: **Wall-Mounted Floating Shelves** (Project #1). Few things deliver more "I made this" payoff than a clean, level row of shelves on a previously bare wall — and you'll see and use them every single day.

**The vitals**
- Time: 2-3 hours
- Budget: 200-400 ILS
- Difficulty: Medium
- Tools: Drill, level, stud finder, drill bits, screwdriver

**All the steps**
1. Find studs in your wall using a stud finder (critical in older Tel Aviv buildings — plaster alone won't hold weight).
2. Measure and mark where shelves will go using a level.
3. Pre-drill holes to avoid cracking plaster.
4. Attach the floating shelf brackets securely into studs.
5. Slide wooden shelves onto the brackets.
6. Test weight capacity *before* loading books on.
7. Decorate with plants, tech books, and minimal accessories.
8. Double-check that the shelves are perfectly level — your inner engineer will thank you.

**Tip for today**: Ace Hardware on Allenby or Obi will have everything you need in one trip. Pick brackets rated for at least 2x the weight you plan to load. Snap a before/after photo — you've earned it.

Have a great build day, Itay!
