# DIY Projects for Tel Aviv Apartment Living

Perfect for a software engineer who wants to spruce up their place with their own hands. These projects range from a quick Saturday morning task to a full weekend build.

---

## 1. Wall-Mounted Floating Shelves

**Description**: Clean, modern shelves for books, plants, and decorative items. Perfect above a desk or living room wall.

- **⏱ Time**: 2-3 hours
- **💰 Budget**: 200-400 ILS
- **🔧 Tools**: Drill, level, stud finder, drill bits, screwdriver
- **📊 Difficulty**: Medium

**Steps**:
1. Find studs in your wall using a stud finder (critical in older Tel Aviv buildings)
2. Measure and mark where shelves will go using a level
3. Pre-drill holes to avoid cracking plaster
4. Attach floating shelf brackets securely into studs
5. Slide wooden shelves onto brackets
6. Test weight capacity before loading books
7. Decorate with plants, tech books, and minimal accessories
8. Ensure shelves are perfectly level (software engineers appreciate precision!)

## 2. Cable Management Desk Setup

**Description**: Organize all your monitors, keyboard, mouse, and charging cables. Keeps your workspace clean and professional.

- **⏱ Time**: 1-2 hours
- **💰 Budget**: 150-300 ILS
- **🔧 Tools**: Scissors, double-sided tape, cable clips, velcro straps
- **📊 Difficulty**: Easy

**Steps**:
1. Gather all cables and identify what goes where
2. Use cable ties or velcro straps to bundle cables by function (power, USB, display)
3. Mount cable clips under desk using adhesive or small screws
4. Route cables through clips and label them with masking tape
5. Use a power strip with surge protection mounted to desk back
6. Create a cable management box for excess cord storage
7. Test all connections after organizing
8. Take a before/after photo (your future self will appreciate it)

## 3. Grow Your Own Herbs - Kitchen Garden

**Description**: Fresh herbs on your balcony or kitchen window. Mint, basil, parsley, coriander — all you need for Tel Aviv cooking.

- **⏱ Time**: 1 hour setup + daily watering
- **💰 Budget**: 250-400 ILS
- **🔧 Tools**: Planters, soil, seeds, watering can, grow light (optional)
- **📊 Difficulty**: Easy

**Steps**:
1. Choose 4-5 herb varieties (basil, mint, parsley, coriander, dill work well)
2. Select planters with drainage holes (10-15cm diameter per plant)
3. Fill planters with potting soil (not garden soil)
4. Plant seeds 1-2cm deep or use seedlings for faster results
5. Water daily in morning (soil should be moist, not soggy)
6. Place on sunny balcony or window (6+ hours sunlight daily)
7. Pinch off flower buds to encourage leaf growth
8. Harvest regularly once plants are 15cm tall — this encourages more growth

**Pro tip**: Israeli herbs thrive in our heat. Water more frequently in summer.

## 4. Custom LED Ambient Lighting

**Description**: Create cinematic mood lighting around your monitor, shelves, or bed. Perfect for Zoom calls and late-night coding sessions.

- **⏱ Time**: 2 hours
- **💰 Budget**: 300-500 ILS
- **🔧 Tools**: LED strip lights, power adapter, voltage converter (if needed), scissors, ruler
- **📊 Difficulty**: Medium

**Steps**:
1. Purchase smart RGB LED strips with app control (Philips Hue or budget alternatives available in Israel)
2. Clean the surface where you'll stick the strip (behind monitor or along shelves)
3. Cut LED strips to desired length using the marked cut points
4. Peel adhesive backing and carefully apply strips
5. Connect power adapter and plug in
6. Download the app and connect via Bluetooth
7. Program lighting scenes (warm white for coding, cool blue for gaming, rainbow for parties)
8. Hide wires with cable management clips

**Pro tip**: USB-powered strips are cheaper and work with most setups. Match your room's color scheme.

## 5. Upcycled Crate Storage

**Description**: Wooden crates become shelving units, storage cubes, or a unique bookshelf. Sustainable and industrial-looking.

- **⏱ Time**: 1-2 hours assembly + 2 hours painting
- **💰 Budget**: 200-350 ILS
- **🔧 Tools**: Wooden crates, paint/stain, brush, screws, drill
- **📊 Difficulty**: Easy

**Steps**:
1. Source wooden crates (check local markets in Tel Aviv, online delivery, or from fruit/wine shops)
2. Clean crates thoroughly with damp cloth
3. Sand any rough edges with medium-grit sandpaper
4. Apply wood stain or paint (2 coats) — choose a color matching your space
5. Let dry completely (overnight)
6. Arrange crates in desired configuration (stacked, wall-mounted, or free-standing)
7. For stability, screw adjacent crates together from inside
8. Add baskets or boxes inside for hidden storage

**Styles**: Modern white, rustic natural wood, or vibrant color-blocked arrangement.

## 6. Phone/Tablet Stand from Wood

**Description**: A simple, elegant stand for video calls, cooking videos, or content consumption. Adjustable angle.

- **⏱ Time**: 1-2 hours
- **💰 Budget**: 80-150 ILS
- **🔧 Tools**: Wood offcuts, saw, sandpaper, drill, screws, paint (optional)
- **📊 Difficulty**: Easy

**Steps**:
1. Cut two pieces of wood (15cm x 5cm) — one vertical, one angled
2. Sand all edges smooth to prevent splinters
3. Create a groove or use a rubber wedge to hold your phone safely
4. Drill holes and use wood screws to join the two pieces at 45-degree angle
5. Add felt pads to the bottom to protect your desk
6. Test stability with your phone in landscape and portrait
7. Optional: paint or stain to match your desk
8. Use for video calls, cooking videos, or as a bedside clock display

**Pro tip**: Add a small groove or rubber strip to the top to prevent phone slipping.

## 7. Window Insulation Film for Summer

**Description**: Reflective film reduces heat and sun glare in your Tel Aviv apartment. Keeps it cooler without blocking views.

- **⏱ Time**: 1-2 hours per window
- **💰 Budget**: 300-500 ILS (for 3-4 windows)
- **🔧 Tools**: Window film, spray bottle, squeegee, utility knife
- **📊 Difficulty**: Medium

**Steps**:
1. Measure your window panes exactly
2. Cut window film slightly larger than panes (trimming is easier than stretching)
3. Mix water with a few drops of dish soap in spray bottle
4. Spray window thoroughly with soapy water
5. Peel backing off film and spray the adhesive side
6. Carefully position film on wet glass, smoothing out from center to edges
7. Use squeegee to remove bubbles and excess water
8. Trim excess with utility knife once dry

**Benefits in Israel**: Reduces AC costs, protects furniture from fading, reduces glare on screens.

## 8. Balcony Privacy Screen

**Description**: Screen off your balcony from neighbors and street view. Great for Tel Aviv apartments in dense neighborhoods.

- **⏱ Time**: 2-3 hours
- **💰 Budget**: 400-700 ILS
- **🔧 Tools**: Wooden frame materials, privacy slats or woven material, drill, screws, saw
- **📊 Difficulty**: Medium

**Steps**:
1. Measure your balcony dimensions
2. Build a simple wooden frame (2x2cm wood, cut to size)
3. Join corners with screws at 90-degree angles
4. Check that frame is square using diagonal measurement
5. Attach privacy slats (horizontal boards) or woven privacy material
6. Secure to balcony railing with screws or brackets
7. Ensure it won't blow away (use additional brackets if needed)
8. Stain or paint if desired

**Options**: Horizontal wood slats (modern look), woven plastic mesh (budget-friendly), or living wall (plant-based privacy).

## 9. Magnetic Knife Strip

**Description**: Keep your cooking knives organized, visible, and accessible. Saves drawer space and looks professional.

- **⏱ Time**: 30 minutes
- **💰 Budget**: 100-200 ILS
- **🔧 Tools**: Magnetic strip, drill, wall anchors, screws, level
- **📊 Difficulty**: Easy

**Steps**:
1. Choose a location in your kitchen (near cutting board, above counter work area)
2. Use a stud finder to locate wall studs for secure mounting
3. Mark mounting holes with a pencil
4. Pre-drill holes to prevent cracking
5. Install wall anchors if not mounting into studs
6. Screw the magnetic strip securely
7. Use a level to ensure it's straight
8. Arrange your knives on the strip (magnetic side should face kitchen)

**Safety note**: Make sure it's secure enough that knives won't fall. Test with light force first.

## 10. DIY Headboard

**Description**: Create a statement headboard for your bed. Can be upholstered fabric, wood slats, or minimalist design.

- **⏱ Time**: 3-4 hours
- **💰 Budget**: 400-800 ILS
- **🔧 Tools**: Wood, fabric (if upholstered), staple gun, nails, drill, saw
- **📊 Difficulty**: Medium

**Steps**:
1. Decide on design (simple plywood panel, wooden slats, or fabric-wrapped)
2. Cut plywood or wood frame to desired height (120-150cm is typical)
3. Sand edges smooth
4. For upholstered: staple fabric to back of plywood, stretching taut
5. For slats: arrange and screw wooden slats across frame
6. Create brackets to mount securely to wall or bed frame
7. Test stability and weight distribution
8. Mount to wall or bed (ensure level and secure)

**Ideas**: White linen fabric for minimalist, wood slats for industrial, bold color for accent wall effect.

## 11. Desk Organizer from PVC Pipe

**Description**: Modular desk storage cubbies made from PVC pipes. Customize size and arrangement for your desk.

- **⏱ Time**: 2 hours
- **💰 Budget**: 150-300 ILS
- **🔧 Tools**: PVC pipes (1.5-2 inch), PVC connectors, saw, sandpaper, paint (optional)
- **📊 Difficulty**: Medium

**Steps**:
1. Plan your organizer layout (grid size and compartments)
2. Cut PVC pipes to identical lengths for horizontal and vertical pieces
3. Sand cut edges smooth
4. Assemble frame using PVC T-connectors and elbows
5. Paint or stain PVC if desired (allows 24 hours drying)
6. Add cross-beams to create compartments
7. Mount to desk using brackets or adhesive strips
8. Fill with pens, cables, papers, and supplies

**Customization**: Add hooks for headphones, baskets for small items, or labels for categories.

## 12. Rope Bookshelf

**Description**: Rustic floating shelves suspended by rope. Looks cool, surprisingly sturdy, perfect for displaying favorites.

- **⏱ Time**: 2-3 hours
- **💰 Budget**: 300-500 ILS
- **🔧 Tools**: Wood planks, thick rope, drill, wood stain/paint, screws, wall anchors
- **📊 Difficulty**: Medium

**Steps**:
1. Choose rope thickness (10-15mm looks best) and natural color or dyed
2. Drill holes in top corners of wood planks (books will rest between rope and plank)
3. Drill corresponding holes in ceiling or high wall points (use stud finder for studs)
4. Thread rope through holes, leaving enough slack for desired shelf height
5. Tie secure knots above each plank (or use carabiners for adjustability)
6. Stain or paint planks if desired
7. Arrange books on shelves, testing weight distribution
8. Ensure rope is tight and level

**Pro tip**: Use hardware store-grade rope for stability. Test with heavy books before loading fully.

## Where to Buy Supplies in Israel

**Hardware & Materials**:
- **Ace Hardware** (Etzal) — Multiple locations in Tel Aviv, good selection of screws, tools, brackets, paint
- **Obi** (Obi.co.il) — Larger selection, online ordering with delivery, good for wood and materials
- **Brico** — Another good hardware chain, competitive prices
- **Local neighborhood hardware stores** (חנות ברזל) — Often have niche items, knowledgeable staff

**Wood & Specialty Materials**:
- **Wood dealers in Lod/Ramla** — Best prices on bulk wood orders
- **Steimatzky** — Books, but also some DIY materials
- **Ali Express / Amazon.il** — LED strips, smart home, tools (slower delivery but cheap)

**Budget-Friendly Options**:
- **Hamashbir Lazarchan** — General store with tools, hardware, supplies
- **Windows and doors suppliers** — Often have leftover wood and materials at discount
- **Local carpenter shops** — Scrap wood, off-cuts, sometimes free or very cheap

**Online Delivery**:
- **Wix stores of individual sellers** — Quality tools, often cheaper than chains
- **Zap.co.il** — Electronics and tools delivery
- **Ravad.net** — Specialized hardware marketplace

**Pro Tip for Tel Aviv**: Weekend markets (Shuk Shabat) in Nahalat Binyamin sometimes have vintage hardware and upcycled materials. Great for budget builds.
