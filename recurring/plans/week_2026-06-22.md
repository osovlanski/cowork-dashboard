## 🎓 Learning Focus

**Month 3 continues — Cloud Security: Encryption & Secrets.**

**Course:** *AWS Security Specialty (SCS-C03)* — Stephane Maarek / Adrian Cantrill

**This week's focus: KMS, encryption, and secrets management (roughly the 40–60% mark of the course)**

- **KMS deep dive** — customer-managed vs. AWS-managed vs. AWS-owned keys, key rotation, and the cost/control trade-offs. Understand *why* you'd manage your own CMK rather than letting AWS do it.
- **Envelope encryption** — the pattern behind almost all at-rest encryption in AWS. Get the data-key / master-key relationship straight; it's the concept the rest of the module assumes.
- **Key policies vs. IAM policies** — this is where this week meets last week's IAM work. Two policy systems guard a KMS key, and knowing how they combine (and how a key policy can lock even an admin out) is the kind of detail the exam — and real incidents — turn on.
- **Secrets Manager vs. Parameter Store** — when each is the right tool, rotation, and pulling secrets at runtime instead of baking them into config.

**Why this now:** Two weeks ago you built the IAM spine; encryption is the natural next layer — IAM decides *who* gets in, KMS decides *what they can read*. The course sequences it this way for a reason, and the two topics reinforce each other. As a senior engineer, skim any "what is encryption" intro and go straight to the KMS mechanics.

*Tip from your own course notes: code along in a throwaway AWS account — create a CMK, encrypt and decrypt an object, then deliberately break the key policy and watch the access fail. You'll remember it far better than any video.*

## 🧳 Weekend Travel Idea

**Haifa & Mount Carmel — gardens, hills, and a cooler coast 🌿**

When Tel Aviv is at full summer intensity, Haifa is a smart move: about an hour by train, set on a hillside that catches the sea breeze, and noticeably more comfortable than the flat coast. Start at the **Baha'i Gardens** — the terraced gardens cascading down the Carmel are worth the trip alone (book the free guided tour slot in advance, mornings are coolest). Wander down through the **German Colony** for brunch on Ben Gurion Boulevard with the gardens framed at the top of the street.

Make a full day of it: drive or bus up into the **Carmel forest** for shade and trails, visit the artists' village of **Ein Hod**, or head to the **Druze villages** of Daliyat al-Karmel for some of the best food in the north. Cool off afterward at one of Haifa's quieter beaches. It's culture, green, and breathable summer air — a genuine reset from the city, and easy to do car-free.
