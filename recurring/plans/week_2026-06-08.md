## 🎓 Learning Focus

**Month 3 begins — Cloud Security.**

**Course:** *AWS Security Specialty (SCS-C03)* — Stephane Maarek / Adrian Cantrill

**This week's focus: IAM Foundations (the first 20–25% of the course)**

- **Identity & access fundamentals** — users, groups, roles, and the difference between identity-based and resource-based policies. This distinction trips up even experienced engineers; nail it first.
- **Policy evaluation logic** — how AWS resolves allow/deny across multiple attached policies, SCPs, and permission boundaries. Understanding the evaluation order is what separates "it works" from "I know why it works."
- **Cross-account access & role assumption** — trust relationships and `sts:AssumeRole`. This is the pattern behind almost every real multi-account setup you'll touch.

**Why this now:** You've spent April and May going from *understanding* LLMs to *shipping* a RAG agent. Month 3 of your 6-month path is deliberately a context switch into AWS Security — and the path is right that security knowledge compounds, so investing early matters. As a senior engineer you can skim any intro material you already know and jump straight to the IAM policy mechanics. Code along in a throwaway AWS account rather than just watching: write a policy, break it, fix it.

*Tip from your own course notes: run videos at 1.5x and skip the basics you already own. Spend the saved time writing real IAM policies.*

## 🔨 DIY Project This Week

**Project: Kitchen Herb Garden 🌿**

- **⏱ Time:** ~1 hour setup, then a few minutes of watering daily
- **💰 Budget:** ₪250–400
- **📊 Difficulty:** Easy

It's June — the perfect month to start herbs that will thrive in the Tel Aviv heat. Set up 4–5 planters (basil, mint, parsley, coriander, dill) with drainage holes on a sunny balcony or windowsill. It's the rare project that keeps giving: fresh herbs for cooking all summer, a bit of green in the apartment, and a small daily ritual (morning watering) that pairs perfectly with your coffee-outside habit.

The setup is genuinely an hour: planters, potting soil (not garden soil), seeds or seedlings, water. Seedlings give you a faster payoff if you want greens within a week. Pinch off flower buds to keep the leaves coming, and water in the morning before the sun is high.

## 🧳 Weekend Travel Idea

**Galilee & Golan — cool water in the heat 💧**

When Tel Aviv hits full summer, the smartest weekend move is north to the water. Head up to the Upper Galilee (about 2.5 hours by car) for a day built around cold springs and shade. Float through the **Majrase (Jordan River Park)** wet trail, kayak a gentle stretch of the **Jordan River**, or walk the shaded path to the **Banias waterfall** in the Hermon Stream reserve — one of the most beautiful and refreshingly cool spots in the country.

Make it an overnight: stay in a cabin (*tzimmer*) near the Hula Valley, eat at a Druze restaurant in the Golan, and catch the sunrise over the valley before the day heats up. It's the antidote to a hot Tel Aviv weekend — green, wet, and a world away from the city.
