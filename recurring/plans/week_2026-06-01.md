## 🎓 Learning Focus

**Course:** *Agentic Workflows with LangChain & LangGraph* — Harrison Chase / LangChain Team → into the **Month 2 capstone project**

**This week's focus:** **Build a RAG-based agent (the Month 2 project milestone)**

You've done the modules. This week is about synthesis, not consumption. Reference the RAG sections of the course as needed, but spend your time building:

- **Ingestion & embeddings:** Load a document set, chunk it sensibly (overlap matters more than people think), embed it into a vector DB (Chroma or FAISS locally — don't over-engineer the infra).
- **Retrieval-augmented generation:** Wire retrieval into your LangGraph agent so it pulls relevant context *before* generating. Watch how chunk size and `k` (number of retrieved docs) change answer quality.
- **Citations & evaluation:** Make the agent cite which chunk each claim came from. Then ask it 5 hard questions and judge the answers honestly — this is where you learn what's actually broken.

**Why this now:** This is the project the 6-month path explicitly calls for at the end of Month 2 ("Build a simple RAG-based agent in LangChain"). It ties together everything since April. Finishing it means you've gone from *understanding* LLMs to *shipping* one — and you'll have a portfolio repo to show for it.

**Concrete challenge:** By Sunday, your repo should let someone clone it, drop in a folder of docs, ask a question, and get a cited answer. Even rough is fine — *done and committed* beats polished-in-your-head.

## 🧳 Weekend Travel Idea

**Akko (Acre) — the old city, by train 🚂**

A genuinely underrated day trip and an easy one: direct-ish train from Tel Aviv (~1.5 hrs), no car needed. Wander the Crusader fortress and underground Templar tunnels, get lost in the Ottoman-era old city, eat hummus at one of the legendary spots (Said or Hummus Said), and walk the sea walls at golden hour. Pair it with fresh fish at the port. It's history, sea, and great food in one relaxed day — perfect for a warm June Saturday.

*Alternative if you want pure beach:* head north to **Beit Yanai** for a quieter, less crowded stretch of coast than the Tel Aviv beaches.
