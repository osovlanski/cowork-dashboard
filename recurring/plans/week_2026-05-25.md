## 🎓 Learning Focus

**Course:** *Agentic Workflows with LangChain & LangGraph* — Harrison Chase / LangChain Team *(Month 2 main track)*

**This week's focus:** **Modules 1–3 — Agent Architectures, State Machines, and Tool Orchestration**

- **Module 1:** Understand *why* LangGraph exists over plain LangChain — the graph model vs. the chain model. This framing click is worth pausing for.
- **Module 2:** State management in agents — how to pass context between tool calls without losing the thread. This is where most agent bugs come from in the wild.
- **Module 3:** Build an agent with 2–3 tools (e.g., a web search tool + a calculator + a memory store). Get the wiring right; don't worry about what the agent actually does yet.

**Why this now:** Fine-tuning (last week) showed you how to *shape* a model. This week you learn how to *orchestrate* one — how to turn a raw model into something with goals, memory, and tools. These two mental models together are the core of production AI engineering. You're building the full picture.

**Concrete challenge:** By Thursday, have a LangGraph agent that can:
1. Take a user question
2. Search for information (mock a search tool if needed)
3. Reason about the result
4. Return a grounded answer

Push it to `llm-learning-2026` on GitHub. It doesn't have to be impressive — it has to be running.

**Tip:** Read the official LangGraph docs alongside the course. Harrison's docs are unusually good; the course and docs complement each other well.

## 🔨 DIY Project This Week

**Project: Rope Bookshelf 📚🪢**

You've been accumulating tech books. It's time to give them a proper home. A rope bookshelf is the rare project that looks intentional and expensive but costs ₪300–500 and takes a Saturday afternoon. The visual payoff is massive.

- **Time:** 2–3 hours total
- **Budget:** ₪300–500
- **Materials:** 2 wood planks (60–80cm wide), thick natural rope (10–15mm diameter, ~4m total), drill with 15–18mm bit, wall anchors and screws, wood stain or paint (optional)
- **Where to buy:** Obi or Ace Hardware for planks + anchors · AliExpress or Zap for thick rope (order mid-week so it arrives by Friday)

**Saturday plan:**
1. Stain or paint planks Friday evening — let dry overnight
2. Saturday morning: drill rope holes in plank corners, find wall studs or install anchors
3. Thread and knot rope, hang planks at your desired heights
4. Load books and a plant — take the photo

This is the kind of project that changes the energy of a room. The drilling takes 20 minutes; the rest is rope work and leveling. Pick a wall you see from your desk.

## ✈️ Travel Idea

**🌊 Rosh HaNikra — Saturday or Friday afternoon**

It's the perfect end-of-May trip: 1.5 hours north on the 2 highway, dramatic white chalk cliffs, sea grottos carved by 10,000 years of waves, and the border with Lebanon a hundred meters away. Nothing else looks like it in Israel.

- **What to do:** Cable car down to the grottos (book in advance — sells out on weekends), walk the cliff trail, swim at the nearby beach if the sea's calm
- **Tip:** Go in the morning to avoid the heat and crowds. Bring water and good shoes for the trail.
- **Pair with:** A stop in Akko (Acre) on the way back — the old city, the market, and the crusader tunnels are 30 minutes from Rosh HaNikra and totally worth it for a coffee and a wander.
- **Cost:** ~₪200–300 total (cable car + gas + food)

This is a genuinely great day. Recommend going with a friend who hasn't been — first-timers are always floored by the grottos.
